Aloo Kachodi

Description

Aloo Kachodi is a famous north indian street food snack recipe. This is a dish mainly cooked during the festive season like diwali but you can cook it anytime your heart desires for it. It consists of a deep-fried dough pocket made from refined flour (maida) that encases a spiced potato filling. The filling typically includes mashed potatoes mixed with ingredients like cumin, coriander, green chilies, and sometimes peas, giving it a savory and slightly spicy taste. It is generally served with green chutney or tomato ketchup which enhances its taste.

Ingredients

  • 2 Boiled Potatoes
  • 1/4 Tsp Red Chilli Powder
  • 1 Tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 Tsp Salt
  • 1/4 Tsp Salt (Yes, again)
  • 1/5 Tsp Garam Masala Powder
  • 1/2 Tsp Fennel Seed Powder
  • 1.5 Cup Whole Wheat Flour
  • 1 Tbsp Oil
  • 250 ml Oil

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, take whole wheat flour and add 1/4 teaspoon salt and 1 tablespoon oil to it. Now with the help of Luke warm water, knead it to a soft and smooth dough.Cover it with a cling film and keep aside for 15 minutes.

  2. In another bowl/plate, mash the boiled potatoes and add all the spices into it. Mix it properly and keep aside. Our stuffing is ready.

  3. Heat sufficient oil in a pan / kadai. Now divide the dough into equal parts and make round balls of it. Take one ball and roll it with the help of rolling pin into a small circle. Place 1 tablespoon of stuffing in the middle and gather all the edges of circle and seal it.

  4. Press it gently with the help of your palm to spread the stuffing, and then again roll it with rolling pin into a circle of 4-4.5 inch diameter.

  5. Put these rolled breads in the hot oil and deep fry on medium heat until they are nice golden in colour from both the sides.

  6. Serve them hot and enjoy with masala chai or green chutney and pickle.
